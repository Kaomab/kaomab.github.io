Information About me

I joined a Samuel Hall research team in 2018 to conduct an end-of-term evaluation for one of their projects. In addition,I was enlisted as an enumerator to conduct surveys in Kalobeyei villages 1, 2, and 3. I enrolled at Kenya College in 2019 and chose to pursue a two-year disaster management curriculum on campus. He received his Disaster Management Diploma in 2021. Kaoma decided to unlock my ICT skills and join Microsoft's official academy in November 2021. He passed the exam with 77% and received a certificate and badge as a Microsoft official academic in computer science. I realized in 2020 that youths struggle to find a scholarship in the Kalobeyei settlement camp because it requires a computer and a good understanding of digital platforms. So, I created an Initiative using online opportunities like free courses and freelancer jobs. As a result, six young women and nine men of Congolese, south Sudanese, Ethiopian, and Somali descent are among the 16 members who have embraced the concept of inclusion and diversity and successfully registered the Kalobeyei Umoja Association. The above is a refugee-led organization that empowers refugees and the host community's youth with computer basics, social entrepreneurship skills, small business management skills, and hygiene and sanitation campaigns. As CEO, I leads the board and focuses on strategies while networking with organizational supporters. In 2021, I became a member of the Global refugee youth Network (GRYN Team), a network that provides capacity-building, project design, implementation, and coordination. In 2022, I designed a Women Refugee Commission-funded project (WRC). The project's impact is felt in the community, where 196 women and 97 young girls participate in the hygiene and sanitation campaign. Concerning preventable disease outbreaks in the Kalobeyei settlement camp, this campaign also encourages community members to practice daily hygiene. During the session, each participant received 500ml of sanitizer and 2 kg of soap. I also conducts an entrepreneurship and computer introductory skill workshop for 282 Kalobeyei youths, 152 girls and 130 boys, at the UN- volunteerism center in village two due to a lack of facilities and materials.
